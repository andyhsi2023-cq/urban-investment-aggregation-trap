### Nature-to-Nature Cities transfer option

Nature offers automatic manuscript transfer to sister journals. If we use this pathway:
- The manuscript is transferred with reviewer comments (if any)
- We should still reformat to NC specifications
- The cover letter should explain why the paper fits NC better than Nature
- **Risk**: NC editors may perceive a transferred paper as "Nature reject" rather than "NC first choice"

**Recommendation**: If desk rejected (no peer review), use the transfer pathway -- it signals that Nature editors considered the paper seriously. If rejected after peer review with negative comments, consider a fresh submission to avoid negative reviewer baggage.

### Cover letter key messages (Nature Cities version)

1. **Why Nature Cities specifically**: "This paper addresses a question at the heart of urban science: whether the statistical conventions used to evaluate urban investment are structurally capable of detecting efficiency decline. The answer -- formalised as the aggregation trap theorem -- has immediate implications for urban governance worldwide."

2. **Carbon dimension**: "We document that below-parity urban investment in China is associated with approximately 2.7 GtCO2 in excess embodied carbon, establishing a direct link between investment misallocation and urban climate outcomes."

3. **Policy framework**: "We propose a differentiated city-level policy framework calibrated to MUQ thresholds, from first-tier cities retaining investment headroom to declining cities requiring managed contraction."

4. **Data contribution**: "The unified panel of 1,567 subnational regions across eight countries and six continents represents, to our knowledge, the largest standardised dataset on urban investment efficiency."
