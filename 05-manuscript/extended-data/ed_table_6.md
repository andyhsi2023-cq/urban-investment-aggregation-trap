**Notes.**
Method A (MUQ flow): uses 5-year moving average (MA5) of MUQ to filter financial-cycle volatility; waste fraction = max(0, 1 - MUQ_MA5) during structural period, raw MUQ during market crash (2021--2024). Method B (Q stock incremental): tracks cumulative excess capital stock K - V when Q_MA5 < 1; annual emissions proportional to incremental excess capital. Method C (composite): simple average of A and B. All methods apply a 50% cap: annual excess emissions cannot exceed 50% of China's total building-sector embodied carbon (estimated at 1,400--1,900 MtCO2/yr). The cap binds in 2022--2024 (marked Y). Monte Carlo uncertainty (10,000 iterations, seed = 20260322): perturbs MUQ (normal, bootstrap SE), carbon intensity (normal, SE = 15%), and Q measurement error (normal, SE = 5%) jointly. Physical cross-validation uses the independent approach of comparing China's urban per-capita housing area (42 m2) against developed-country benchmarks (33 m2) and applying embodied carbon intensities from Zheng et al. (2024). The composite estimate (2.97 GtCO2) and physical estimate (3.78 GtCO2) agree within a factor of 1.3, providing order-of-magnitude validation.
