# Carbon Literature Patch — P1 #4 + P2 #11

Addresses Reviewer C's comment that carbon estimates are disconnected from the building embodied carbon literature. Adds IPCC AR6 WG3 Ch.9, UNEP GlobalABC, Zhong et al. (2021), IEA (2023), and Creutzig et al. (2016) references, and contextualises the 5.3 GtCO2 estimate against global building-sector emissions.

---

## Patch 1: Results F3 Para 4 — Insert before "They also exclude the global dimension"

Context: The sentence currently reads "These estimates represent a conservative lower bound. They cover construction-phase embodied carbon only, excluding operational energy consumption of excess building stock, the opportunity cost of capital diverted from potentially lower-carbon sectors, and any demolition or redevelopment emissions. They also exclude the global dimension..."

Insert the following (~60 words) after "...any demolition or redevelopment emissions." and before "They also exclude the global dimension":

> To place the estimate in context: global building-sector emissions total approximately 10 GtCO2 per year, of which embodied carbon in new construction accounts for 3.5--4.0 GtCO2 annually [UNEP GlobalABC, 2022; IPCC, 2022]. China contributes roughly half of global building embodied carbon [Zhong et al., 2021]. Our cumulative 5.3 GtCO2 estimate thus corresponds to approximately 1.5 years of global building embodied emissions -- indicating that even the fraction of construction driven by value-eroding investment carries a globally significant carbon footprint.

## Patch 2: Discussion Para 4 — Insert before "Integrating investment efficiency metrics"

Context: The paragraph currently reads "...Peak annual excess emissions in 2024 (1,714 MtCO2) coincide with the MUQ nadir, suggesting that carbon waste accelerates nonlinearly as marginal returns approach zero. Integrating investment efficiency metrics into carbon accounting frameworks could, in principle, identify avoidable embodied emissions before construction begins rather than after carbon is irreversibly committed to the atmosphere."

Insert the following (~40 words) after "...as marginal returns approach zero." and before "Integrating investment efficiency metrics":

> This framing aligns with the "Avoid-Shift-Improve" mitigation hierarchy advocated by the IEA and UNEP GlobalABC [IEA, 2023; IPCC, 2022; Creutzig et al., 2016]: MUQ provides an ex ante screening tool for the "Avoid" tier, flagging construction whose expected asset-value return is insufficient to justify its embodied carbon commitment.
