建议添加到论文 Methods 的文字 (P2 #7):
------------------------------------------------------------
To address concerns about compositional bias in the unbalanced Chinese city
panel (20 cities in 2011 versus 213 in 2016), we re-estimated the core
MUQ-investment relationship on balanced subpanels. In the 2015-2016 balanced
panel (51 cities with valid MUQ in both years), the OLS coefficient remains
negative and directionally consistent with the full-sample estimate
(beta = -4.55, 95% CI [-6.74, -2.36], p < 10^-4). Cross-sectional regressions
for 2015 and 2016 individually yield negative coefficients (beta_2015 = -4.39,
p = 0.006; beta_2016 = -2.17, p < 10^-3). The 2013-2016 balanced panel
(49 cities observed in all four years) produces similar results
(beta = -3.92, 95% CI [-5.52, -2.33], p < 10^-6). The sign and approximate
magnitude of the investment-efficiency gradient are robust to balancing the
panel; indeed, the balanced-panel estimates are larger in absolute value than
the full-sample beta of -2.23, suggesting that compositional entry of smaller
cities in later years attenuates rather than inflates the estimate.

建议添加到论文 Methods 的文字 (P2 #8):
------------------------------------------------------------
Because ACS 5-Year estimates incorporate four overlapping survey years,
standard HC1 errors may understate uncertainty due to serial correlation.
We re-estimated the core US regression using Newey-West standard errors
(lag = 4, matching the ACS overlap window). The Newey-West SE is 1.15x
the HC1 SE; the coefficient remains highly significant (beta = +2.75,
Newey-West p < 10^-155). Results are also robust to clustering by MSA
(SE ratio = 1.29, p < 10^-124) and by year (SE ratio = 6.28, p < 10^-6).
Even the most conservative standard error (year-clustered, which inflates
SE by 6.3x due to only 12 year clusters) preserves significance at
p < 10^-6. The ACS overlap does not materially affect inference.

------------------------------------------------------------
建议添加到论文 Methods 的文字 (P2 #9):
------------------------------------------------------------
The paper reports 25 hypothesis tests across three main findings. To control
the false discovery rate, we applied Benjamini-Hochberg (BH) correction at
alpha = 0.05. All core findings survive FDR correction: the three
within-group Simpson's paradox tests (adjusted q = 0.003, 0.003, 0.005),
the China city-level OLS beta (q < 10^-6), and the US MSA beta (q < 10^-6).
22 of 25 tests remain significant after BH correction. The three that do not
are the high-income group (which was reported as non-significant), the China
within-estimator (reported as marginal, p = 0.063), and the parallel-trends
diagnostic (reported as marginal, p = 0.093). We also verified robustness
under the more conservative Bonferroni correction, where 15 of 25 tests
remain significant. No originally significant test loses significance under
BH-FDR correction.
